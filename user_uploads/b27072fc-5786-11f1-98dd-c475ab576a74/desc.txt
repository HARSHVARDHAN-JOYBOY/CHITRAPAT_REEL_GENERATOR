You have a right to perform your prescribed duties, but you are not entitled to the fruits of your actions. Never consider yourself the cause of the results of your activities, nor be attached to inaction  Let your concern be with the action alone, and never with the fruits of action. Do not let the results of your actions be your motive, and do not be attached to inaction. By performing one’s natural duties, the individual gradually purifies the mind.  Perform your duty and abandon all attachment to success or failure. Such evenness of mind is called yoga. Acting in accordance with one’s duty without attachment to the outcomes, while maintaining equanimity in success and failure, leads to the Supreme.  It is better to live your own destiny imperfectly than to live an imitation of somebody else’s life with perfection. Karmanye wadhikaraste maa faleshu kadachanh maa karmheturbhu matesagostav karmani .
